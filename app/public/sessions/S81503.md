## Abstract
Quantum chemistry has long been the gold standard for molecular accuracy—and the bottleneck for scale. This session presents EXESS, a GPU-native quantum chemistry platform that establishes the state of the art of quantum chemistry on GPUs. Building on ACM Gordon Bell Prize–winning work, EXESS achieved record double-precision floating-point performance and scaled electronic-structure methods to exascale on tens of thousands of GPUs. These advances transform quantum-accurate biomolecular simulation from an academic endeavour into a platform capability. We highlight the scaling principles behind EXESS and show how floating-point emulation on NVIDIA Blackwell accelerates double-precision-critical workloads, enabling routine generation of quantum-accurate data for AI model training, accelerated drug and discovery, and automated, in-silico molecular design workflows.
