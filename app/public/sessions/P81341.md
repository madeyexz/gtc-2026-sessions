## Abstract
Cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) contribute to over one-fourth of all deaths in India, making early and equitable risk screening a priority. Screening methods relying on blood pressure and risk charts have low sensitivity and detect disease late, while advanced ultrasound tools are confined to tertiary centers by high cost, complexity, and legal constraints. We propose a low-cost, ultrafast A-mode ultrasound edge system powered by automated deep learning (DL) algorithms for arterial wall detection and motion tracking. The system enables multi-marker vascular assessment — including arterial stiffness, endothelial function, and calibration-free blood pressure — at the point of care. Compact, intelligent, and easy to use, the technology promotes field-deployable cardiovascular screening while building indigenous vascular phenotyping datasets to drive personalized and preventive healthcare.
