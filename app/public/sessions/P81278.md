## Abstract
In real factories, safety regulations, security restrictions, and a lack of skilled operators make it difficult to collect and deploy motion data at scale. To address these challenges, we developed an intuitive teleoperation pipeline using a single consumer-grade head-mounted display (HMD), removing the need for external trackers or multi-camera systems. The system integrates Jetson-based inference with an RTOS-driven dual-network control architecture—EtherCAT for ALICE-M1 and RS-485 for ALICE4—enabling deterministic real-time operation. A GR00T-Mimic-inspired data augmentation method further diversifies robot trajectories without additional data collection, enhancing robustness across manipulation tasks. Experiments in industrial scenarios show stable autonomous execution and confirm the feasibility of deploying Jetson-based humanoid systems for real-world industrial applications.
