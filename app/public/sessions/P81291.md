## Abstract
AI is transforming medical imaging and surgical navigation by enhancing precision, reducing human error, and improving patient outcomes. Yet, its integration in operating rooms is challenging due to stringent real-time and safety demands.

In minimally invasive procedures such as arthroscopy, limited maneuverability and indirect visualization make tasks like ACL reconstruction technically demanding. AI-driven video-based surgical navigation in edge computing systems offer improved accuracy and efficiency without high-end workstations.

Our recent collaboration with Smith & Nephew features an edge AI surgical assistance pipeline built on the NVIDIA Holoscan framework and Jetson AGX Orin hardware. We optimized three AI subsystems — segmentation, classification, and lens mark detection — validated on cadaveric datasets, achieving real-time performance with dozens of frames per second.
