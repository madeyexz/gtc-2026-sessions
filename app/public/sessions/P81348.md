## Abstract
The limited part-level understanding capability of existing 2D semantic segmentation models is a bottleneck in 2D-to-3D part segmentation. So we introduce cross-view automated part alignment (CAPA), a novel zero-shot multi-view framework that systematically unifies state-of-the-art 2D and 3D models to achieve superior, class-agnostic 3D part segmentation. It utilizes ControlNet and Semantic-SAM to generate and segment 36 geometrically consistent, high-fidelity 2D part masks across multiple views. Next, it employs PTv3 for feature extraction and a novel Wasserstein distance-based clustering approach for cross-view integration, ensuring consistent 3D part definition. CAPA achieved 15% class-agnostic mIoU improvement over existing methods on challenging benchmarks: PartObjaverse-Tiny and PartNetE. And it only requires 5-9 minutes per object on an RTX 3090 (vs. 15-20 min for other models).
