## Abstract
ERAS protocols need real-time, personalized support; CPU rule engines have high latency and low throughput. We quantify performance and clinical utility of GPU-accelerated LLM inference for ERAS decision support by building a multi-agent LLM system on a single RTX 5090 with hybrid RAG, benchmarked against a CPU rules baseline across three ERAS use cases: PONV prevention, POD checklists, and chest-tube removal. Evaluations included microbenchmarks, concurrency stress tests, and task-level accuracy.

Results: Mean latency fell 2.9× (1900→650 ms); P95 fell 2.4× (2050→850 ms); max throughput rose 7× (0.5→3.5 QPS). Under 16-patient concurrency, P95 remained ~2.1 s with GPU utilization 60–80% and VRAM 16–18 GB. Task accuracy reached 95%.

A single RTX 5090 enables bedside, sub-second responses and supports 8–16 concurrent patients, offering a practical solution with a favorable cost profile.
