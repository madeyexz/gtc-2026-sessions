## Speakers
### Siteng Ma (Author)

## Abstract
Our GPU-accelerated pipeline bridges first-principles data generation, neural potential training, and large-scale atomistic simulation. Starting from automated data generation, representative chemical systems are selected and explored through DFT calculations under an active-learning strategy. Then neural interatomic potentials are trained from scratch or refined via multi-head fine-tuning and model distillation, leveraging GPU-optimized cuequivariant architectures for efficient learning. The trained models are deployed in LAMMPS-MLIAP for massive-scale molecular dynamics, achieving 300,000 atom simulations with full GPU acceleration. This framework unifies data, training, and simulation within a single reproducible ecosystem—transforming traditional materials modeling into a scalable, automated workflow capable of continuously improving its accuracy through iterative GPU-based learning.
