## Abstract
As AI factories move from experimentation to production, teams quickly learn that GPU performance alone does not determine inference capacity, latency, or user experience. Inference workloads produce long-lived, bursty, stateful traffic that strains traditional networking and security architectures, reducing token throughput, increasing time-to-first-token, and introducing instability at scale.

This session explores how AI factories built on NVIDIA accelerated computing benefit from BlueField DPU-accelerated networking and cloud-native traffic management. We examine the unique demands of AI inference, how NVIDIA DPUs offload and isolate critical data paths, and how inference-aware traffic control improves performance, reduces latency, and enables consistent multi-tenant inference services in production environments.
