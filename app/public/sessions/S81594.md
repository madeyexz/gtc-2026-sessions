## Abstract
We introduce a GPU-accelerated system for decomposing engagement into Brand (persistent equity), Fame (spatial reach), and Virality (temporal dynamics). Standard analysis cannot separate these effects because they are mathematically entangled: observing any two determines the third. We address this by using spatio-temporal causal graphs that explicitly encode influence structure, with CUDA kernels for CSR propagation and distributed Shapley computation across GPU clusters.
Built on an Online Evolving Spike Neural Network distributed pipeline, the system uses variance-optimal stratified sampling for coalition evaluation and processes 100,000+ marketing time series at daily resolution via custom CUDA kernels and C++ simulation bridges. The framework enables teams to identify whether observed variation is driven by content quality, audience segmentation, or timing effects—separating forces that traditional attribution methods conflate. This turns engagement decomposition into actionable budget allocation grounded in large-scale causal inference.
