## Abstract
AI literacy has moved from a "nice-to-have" to a readiness requirement. The open question is execution: how schools build access that scales, supports educators, and reaches students across different learning paths, geographies, and resources.

This panel brings together education leaders and practitioners shaping that execution from four angles: classroom and school implementation, learning science and workforce-relevant skill-building, platform-level delivery at scale, and institutional systems that connect education to long-term opportunity.

Together, the panel will share practical approaches to expand AI literacy and access without turning schools into pilot sites. You'll leave with a clear view of what to teach, how to equip educators, how to deploy tools responsibly, and how to build pathways that connect early literacy to real-world readiness over time.
