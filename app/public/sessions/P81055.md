## Abstract
We demonstrate how the Density Matrix Embedding Theory with Variational Quantum Eigensolver (DMET–VQE) can be applied to uncover topological properties in the one-dimensional Su–Schrieffer–Heeger–Hubbard (SSHH) model, and how its computational performance can be enhanced through asynchronous simulation across multiple GPUs using NVIDIA CUDA-Q. In our simulations, the open-boundary edge-state signatures — that is, the signals of the topological states splitting away from half filling to one-and-three-quarters filling as onsite interaction increases — depend on the fragment size, which determines the number of qubits, revealing the significance of computational acceleration in high-qubit simulations.
