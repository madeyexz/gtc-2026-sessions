## Abstract
Optimizing attention on NVIDIA Blackwell demands more than a monolithic kernel to handle diverse models, training, inference, and RL workloads. We introduce Magic Attention, a high-performance, composable framework built on CUTLASS/CuTe that navigates the vast scheduling design space of the FlashAttention algorithm on the NVIDIA Blackwell architecture. We discuss four different forward attention kernel schedules, leveraging two Tensor Memory (TMEM) partitionings for deep pipeline overlap, and convolution-inspired runtime tensor packing. We will also discuss the use of softmax emulation and correction skipping.
