## Abstract
Explore how we enable real-time, near-DNS-quality reconstruction of urban airflow using only sparse, strategically optimized sensors, along with interpretable sensor layouts that cities can actually deploy. Our approach offers new tools for understanding how wind moves pollutants and heat through dense urban spaces, helping communities make faster and better-informed decisions. This work is especially timely as the World Health Organization reports that air pollution contributes to 7 million premature deaths every year, underscoring the urgent need for accurate, scalable monitoring solutions that support healthier and more resilient cities.
