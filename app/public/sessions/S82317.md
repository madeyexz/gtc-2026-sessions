## Abstract
As AI workloads scale, infrastructure complexity increases — across power, cooling, networking, and compute. For customers deploying large GPU clusters, the question is no longer just about access to hardware, but about how reliably and predictably that infrastructure can be delivered and operated.
This session examines the role of vertical integration in building AI cloud environments at scale. IREN develops its own sites, secures grid interconnections, engineers high-voltage substations, designs and constructs data centers, and deploys and operates its own GPU clusters — all within a single operating platform.

By controlling the full stack — from power infrastructure through to compute — the platform reduces dependency on third parties, shortens deployment timelines, and provides tighter alignment between facility design and cluster architecture.
