## Abstract
We present the Galaxea Open-World Dataset, a large and diverse collection of robotic behaviors captured in real human environments. Demonstrations are collected with a consistent robotic embodiment and annotated with precise subtask-level language descriptions for both training and evaluation. Building on this dataset, we introduce G0, a dual-system framework combining a vision-language model (VLM) for multi-modal planning and a vision-language-action (VLA) model for fine-grained execution. G0 is trained through a three-stage curriculum: cross-embodiment pre-training, single-embodiment pre-training, and task-specific post-training. Extensive benchmarks on tabletop manipulation, few-shot learning, and long-horizon mobile manipulation validate the approach, showing that single-embodiment pre-training, together with the Galaxea Dataset, is crucial for strong overall performance.
