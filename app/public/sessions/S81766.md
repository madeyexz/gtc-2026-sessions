## Abstract
The landscape of experimental science is being reshaped by the demand for real-time insight at the petabyte scale. With the Vera C. Rubin Observatory’s Legacy Survey of Space and Time (LSST) Camera and the Linac Coherent Light Source II (LCLS II) at SLAC National Accelerator Laboratory, data are arriving at unprecedented speed and volume.
 
NVIDIA-accelerated computing, coupled with GPU-accelerated Python libraries—CuPy, cuPyNumeric—are making possible what was previously out of reach. Now, by running these same scientific analysis pipelines on NVIDIA DGX Grace Hopper and Blackwell, Spark, and RTX PRO, researchers are gaining powerful new advantages for both performance and collaboration. Many months of data analysis compresses to a few hours through cleverly solved equations using distributed computation on GPUs.
