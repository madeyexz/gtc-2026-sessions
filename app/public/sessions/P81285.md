## Abstract
We demonstrate how systematically reusing and fine-tuning discriminators from generative models yields robust deepfake detection across diverse synthesis methods. By integrating scale-adaptive features from ProGAN discriminators with temporal-consistency features from diffusion models, our GRAVEX framework achieves 74.33% F1-score across nine state-of-the-art generators—outperforming conventional ViT-based detectors by over 30% F1, on average. Implemented using NVIDIA MONAI for streamlined training and deployment, this approach addresses the critical challenge of detecting AI-generated faces as generative AI becomes increasingly sophisticated. On challenging datasets like CIFAKE, where baseline detectors fail completely, GRAVEX maintains 88% F1-score, demonstrating exceptional robustness and validating discriminator reuse as a promising path toward production-ready detection systems.
