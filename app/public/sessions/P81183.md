## Abstract
We explore a real-time execution stack that unifies NVIDIA Isaac Robot Foundation Models, ROS2, and software-based motion control to achieve sub-millisecond, deterministic performance — enabling precise, synchronized actions in the real world and closing the sim-to-real gap in physical AI. We also introduce sensor-driven fine-tuning methods to improve real-world adaptability through residual policy learning and LoRA-based adaptation.
