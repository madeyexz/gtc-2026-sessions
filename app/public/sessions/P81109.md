## Abstract
We explore how disaggregated inference optimizes GPU resource utilization in real-world reasoning and AI agent use cases within telco environments. We also analyze the workload characteristics of Wikipedia-based AI agents, which increase prefill inputs. To handle such large inputs, we experimented with the NVIDIA Dynamo framework on NVIDIA H100 GPUs in a disaggregated setup. Our results show an approximate 16% overhead in time-to-first-token and confirm superior stability under high loads, compared to aggregated inference.

From a telecom perspective, the disaggregated inference could represent a significant breakthrough to enable flexible, on-demand GPU allocation across multi–data center architectures and supports scalable AI agent deployment. Future work will focus on developing cost models for geo-distributed GPU resources to ensure efficient resource planning for our customers.
