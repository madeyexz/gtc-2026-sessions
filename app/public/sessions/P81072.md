## Abstract
We introduce a Quantum Walk-based Adaptive Distribution Generator (QW-ADG), a hybrid quantum-classical framework designed for high-efficiency generative modeling. The key is using an entangled two-qubit coin within a variational quantum walk, generating complex 2D spatial correlations for tasks like image creation.

Implemented on the CUDA-Q platform and accelerated on an NVIDIA GPU, our model demonstrates a significant performance advantage over alternative quantum approaches. In a direct benchmark generating 8x8 MNIST digits, our QW-ADG is over 10x faster than a standard quantum generative adversarial network (QGAN) while achieving comparable or superior accuracy (L1 Error).

We establish the QW-ADG as a highly efficient and practical framework for near-term quantum generative modeling, highlighting the power of structured quantum dynamics combined with high-performance simulation.
