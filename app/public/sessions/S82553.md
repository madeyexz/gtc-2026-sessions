## Abstract
AI has crossed a threshold. Autonomous agents like OpenClaw, no longer just assist but hold credentials, persist context across sessions, spawn subagents, and execute continuously against production systems. That's a fundamentally different threat model than anything enterprise security was built to handle. The attack surface is real. Every prompt injection is a potential credential leak. Every third-party skill is an unreviewed binary. Every major enterprise platform is becoming an agent platform, and the governance infrastructure to match that reality doesn't exist at scale yet.
This panel brings together security and AI leaders to ask the hard questions: what does a real security model for autonomous agents look like? 
The agents are ready. The infrastructure to trust them is what we're here to discuss.
