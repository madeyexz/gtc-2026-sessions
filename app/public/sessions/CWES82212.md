## Speakers
### Greg Kimball (CWE Team Lead)
> Gregory Kimball is a software engineering manager at NVIDIA working on the RAPIDS team. Gregory leads development for libcudf, the CUDA/C++ library for columnar data processing that powers RAPIDS cuDF. Gregory holds a PhD in applied physics from the California Institute of Technology.

### Divye Paresh Gala (CWE Expert)
> Divye Gala is a Senior Software Engineer on the RAPIDS team at NVIDIA, developing fast & scalable algorithms for machine learning and graph analytics on the GPU. Divye holds Ms. and Bs. degrees in Computer Science. In his free time, Divye enjoys playing soccer and watching cricket.

### Vyas Ramasubramani (CWE Expert)
> Vyas leads the development of Python tabular data processing in RAPIDS, including libraries like cudf-polars and cudf.pandas. Vyas has extensive experience across the CUDA development stack in Python, C, and C++, as well as with packaging CUDA-accelerated libraries.

### Bobby Evans (CWE Expert)
> Bobby Evans has worked on open-source big data for well over a decade. He is a PMC member and committer for the Apache Spark, Hadoop, Storm and TEZ projects. Most recently he has worked on the RAPIDS Accelerator for Apache Spark to reduce cost of ETL processing using GPUs.

## Abstract
Drop into these walk-up office hours to meet the experts building NVIDIA’s CUDA-X data science libraries and talk through your end-to-end pipeline needs. ​Get practical guidance on where cuDF, cuML, cuGraph, and GPU-accelerated Spark can boost productivity in your workflow. ​Discuss how to experiment and scale from DGX Spark to the largest supercomputers, and share feedback that can help shape upcoming releases.

Important: Connect With the Experts sessions are interactive sessions that give you a unique opportunity to meet, in either a group or one-on-one setting, with the minds behind NVIDIA’s products and research to get your questions answered. Attendees can meet with experts on a first-come, first-served basis.
