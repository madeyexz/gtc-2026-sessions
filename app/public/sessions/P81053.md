## Abstract
Dual engines of thought (DEoT) — an adaptive reasoning framework that dynamically orchestrates breadth and depth reasoning modules to enable stable, efficient, and context-sensitive inference in large language models. DEoT employs an entropy-regulated controller to judiciously navigate the reasoning space, mitigating both informational sparsity and computational overhead. This dynamic coordination facilitates robust multi-source integration and enhances the model's capacity for deep causal inference. Empirical results on the open-ended News-to-Question benchmark reveal that DEoT significantly outperforms standard LLMs, delivering responses that are not only more analytically nuanced, but also more logically coherent. Our architecture demonstrates broad applicability across high-impact domains, including biomedicine, economics, geopolitics, industrial systems, and advanced technologies.
