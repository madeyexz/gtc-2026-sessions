## Abstract
A key challenge in many scientific tasks is inferring the underlying scientific phenomenon from sparse and corrupted measurements, also known as inverse problems. A canonical example is imaging a black hole using the Event Horizon Telescope, which collects sparse radio waves that have been corrupted by the Earth's atmosphere. We have developed a framework based on diffusion models that can solve a broad range of scientific inverse problems, with applications that span medical imaging, astronomy, seismology, and climate science.
