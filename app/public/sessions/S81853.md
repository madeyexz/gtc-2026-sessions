## Abstract
Coherent NVIDIA CPU-GPU systems provide a versatile platform to support a wide spectrum of workloads. However not all jobs require all capabilities at scale. Co-scheduling CPU-only, partially accelerated, and fully GPU-accelerated jobs on NVIDIA CPU-GPU nodes can boost throughput and energy efficiency, removing the need for multiple dedicated clusters. We discuss isolation mechanisms including CPU affinity via cgroups, cache and memory bandwidth partitioning through ARM’s MPAM extensions, and InfiniBand QoS for network traffic separation. Learn to identify co-schedule candidates and assess scheduling policy design options and incentives for user adoption. We'll present workload studies aligned with NERSC’s upcoming Doudna system, demonstrating projected improvements in utilization and efficiency while highlighting open challenges in enabling fine-grained resource sharing.
