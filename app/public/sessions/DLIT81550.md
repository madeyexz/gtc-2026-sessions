## Abstract
In this hands-on lab, we will create adaptive, tool-using AI agents that learn through trial, error, and feedback, moving beyond static prompting. We’ll work with NVIDIA NeMo Gym and NeMo RL open libraries, and post-train Nemotron open models to build domain-specific agents capable of mastering complex workflows and tool-use using reinforcement learning (RL).
 
As a practical and engaging challenge, we’ll build and train an agent to play a “Wordle” game. While the game is lighthearted, attendees experience the same enterprise-grade stack and development patterns that can scale to real-world applications such as autonomous coding, applied reasoning, scientific discovery, and to any custom use case.
Together, we’ll walk through the full RL lifecycle - from building custom simulations/ RL environments and designing verifiable rewards to iterative training and performance evaluation - developing the foundations needed to build high-performance, domain AI agents that continually learn and adapt.
 
Prerequisite(s):


Basic experience with Python


A general understanding of LLMs or agent workflows or Generative AI concepts (no deep ML background required)


Familiarity with REST APIs or function-calling concepts is helpful but not mandatory


Optional but beneficial: Exposure to NVIDIA NeMo, containerized development (Docker), or cloud workflows


No prior experience with Nemotron models or NeMo is required.
