## Abstract
Hyperparameter optimization (HPO) can unlock the full potential of deep learning (DL) models, but it's one of the most compute-intensive tasks in DL. Existing HPO parallelization strategies underutilize powerful GPUs, especially for training lightweight deep neural networks. Resource-sharing mechanisms such as multi-process service can improve GPU utilization, but naive adaptations for HPO workloads lead to poor performance. We present HyperSack: a distributed HPO framework for dynamic and resource-aware scheduling on GPU-based HPC systems. HyperSack cuts the execution time of HPO workloads by orchestrating efficient placement of DL training jobs. Our tests on vision and language models HPO workloads show up to 2.8x performance improvement in execution time on AI00s, 4.0x on HI00s, and 3.9x on a combination of AI00s and HI00s using HyperSack over standard HPO parallelization methods.
