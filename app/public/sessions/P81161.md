## Abstract
Since industrial robots in real factories perform a variety of tasks — including welding, inspection, and pick-and-place — the recently developed digital twin framework should be applicable to all tasks for practical on-site deployment. In this study, we collaborated with Hyundai Motors to verify the scalability of the digital twin framework by applying it not only to the welding shop line, but also to vision inspection and depalletizing tasks. The motion planning results for each task were deployed onsite, and it was verified that the engineering time can be reduced by over 88% without compromising productivity. These results confirm that the proposed framework can quickly adapt to diverse robotic workflows, improving performance and scalability across real production lines.
