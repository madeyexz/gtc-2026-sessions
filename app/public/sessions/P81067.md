## Abstract
Learning from videos is a path toward generalist robots by leveraging diverse visual data beyond traditional datasets. Videos often contain recurring skills (e.g., grasping, lifting) across tasks and environments. While skill-based methods can learn reusable behaviors, they usually require clean, action-labeled data. Conversely, existing video-learning methods often train monolithic models or focus on single-step dynamics, hindering skill composition and long-horizon planning. We propose Skill Abstraction from Optical Flow (SOF), which learns skills directly from action-free videos. SOF uses optical flow as a surrogate for action and adapts skill learning to operate on flow-based representations. It plans in skill space and translates these plans into actions. SOF improves multitask and long-horizon performance, enabling skill acquisition and composition directly from raw visual data.
