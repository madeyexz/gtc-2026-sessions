## Abstract
Dementia affects over 55 million people worldwide and is expected to double by 2050. Diagnosis and prognosis are challenging due to reliance on expert interpretation of neuroimaging and clinical data. Large vision-language models (VLMs) offer a promising solution by integrating MRI images and textual information to generate clinically meaningful image captions and diagnostic reasoning, but their potential in brain imaging is limited by the scarcity of large-scale, paired MRI-report datasets. While public datasets such as ADNI [2] provide extensive imaging and clinical data for Alzheimer’s disease study, they often lack detailed radiological reports linking visual and linguistic features. 

We develop Brain Retrieval-Assisted Diagnosis (BrainRAD), an AI-driven framework designed to advance precision diagnosis in dementia through integrating quantitative MRI biomarkers and large VLMs.
