## Abstract
Most extract, transform, load (ETL) workflows pull data out of object storage into client machines or servers for preprocessing, augmentation, or custom transformations. This lacks scalability, and unnecessary data movement often degrades performance significantly. Unlike other ETL tools, in-storage ETL performs transformations on the same machines where your data lives, minimizing redundant transfers by exploiting data locality.

We introduce an in-storage ETL system that runs I/O-intensive transformations close to the data. It supports inline transformations for real-time training, and offline batch processing where results are stored in a destination bucket. We’ll explore its architecture and benefits, highlighting linear scalability and high performance. Learn how to accelerate ETL pipelines, streamline workflows, and scale seamlessly from a real-world example of data preprocessing.
