## Abstract
Enterprises are moving beyond prompt engineering toward repeatable, data-driven customization loops. This technical session explores how Aible and Yum! Brands transitioned NVIDIA Nemotron 3 from general-purpose models into specialized domain experts.
Aible demonstrates a Txt2SQL data flywheel designed for highly regulated environments, utilizing SME feedback and data augmentation to drive query accuracy and strict compliance. Yum! Brands details their approach to agentic tool-calling for high-volume ordering (e.g., Taco Bell), leveraging Small Language Models (SLMs) and SDG-style reasoning traces to improve tool-use disambiguation and execution latency.
Arijit Sengupta (CEO & Founder, Aible), Rocky Yost (Head of AI, Yum! Brands) and NVIDIA walk through these production data flywheels built with NVIDIA NeMo microservices. Learn how to design evaluation-led iteration cycles and determine when to use specialized SLMs versus full-scale model alignment for your own domain-specific use cases.
